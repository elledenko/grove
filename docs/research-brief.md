# Grove — Research Brief

## Market Overview

The mental wellness app market is valued at ~$7B (2025) and growing 15-20% annually. Gamified self-care is a proven sub-category — Finch alone does $12M/year with 5M+ downloads. Users are increasingly seeking non-clinical, low-pressure mental health tools.

## Competitor Analysis

| App | Model | Strength | Weakness |
|-----|-------|----------|----------|
| **Finch** | Virtual pet bird + self-care tasks | Emotional attachment, gentle tone, 4.8 stars | Mobile-only, childish tone, slow UX, $70/yr Android |
| **Habitica** | RPG habit tracker | Deep gamification, social guilds | Complex, intimidating, no wellness focus |
| **Forest** | Focus timer → virtual trees | Simple, satisfying | Narrow use case (focus only) |
| **Bearable** | Health/mood tracking | Comprehensive data | Clinical feel, no gamification |
| **Wysa** | AI therapy chatbot | Conversational CBT | No gamification, no companion |

## Gap Analysis

1. **No web version** — Finch is mobile-only; web access is a top user request
2. **Adult aesthetic gap** — Finch's cutesy tone alienates 25-40 demographic
3. **UX friction** — Too many taps/animations between task completion
4. **Scheduling rigidity** — Assumes daily routines; fails shift workers and irregular schedules
5. **No AI personalization** — Static recommendations regardless of mood patterns

## Target User

- Adults 22-40 who want self-care habits but find clinical apps sterile and cute apps childish
- Remote workers, knowledge workers with flexible/irregular schedules
- People with mild anxiety, burnout, or ADHD seeking gentle external motivation
- Users who want web access alongside mobile

## Go/No-Go: GO

Strong market, proven mechanic (virtual companion), clear gaps to exploit (web-first, adult tone, faster UX). The plant companion angle differentiates from pet-based competitors.
